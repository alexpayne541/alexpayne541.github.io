Build Story

I originally bought this 1978 Camaro with my grandfather, who was a professional paint and body master. We planned to build this car together, but he passed away before we could begin. I am now building it in his honor. He was going to teach me the skill and art of the trade, but instead I am learning on my own with the help of YouTube and a lot of trial and error. When I bought the car, it was a fully stripped-out shell with no paperwork. It was rust-free and a true Z28, so I took a chance on it. The first step was securing paperwork through a lien sale, and once that was done, it was all hands on deck to begin the build. The car had been stored indoors in California for decades after being disassembled by a previous owner who intended to turn it into a drag racing car. He never got around to it, so he sold it to a local Oregonian who broke the cardinal rule: he didn’t get his wife’s permission first. So the Camaro sat once again until he finally sold it — and that’s when it became mine.
